### A1 â€” The Efficiency Staircase: What Each Round Requires

Each round of the tournament has a distinct efficiency floor. Teams that fail to meet the threshold for their current round exit â€” not because they had a bad game, but because the opponent's quality exposes structural weaknesses that worked fine against weaker competition.

| Stage | Min AdjEM | Min AdjD Rank | Min AdjO Rank | Key Differentiator |
|-------|-----------|---------------|---------------|--------------------|
| Win R64 | +8 to +10 | Any (defense helps, not required for 1 win) | Any | Single-game variance; matchup luck sufficient |
| Win R32 | +14 to +16 | Top 55 preferred | Top 55 | Preparation advantage goes to efficient teams |
| Reach Sweet 16 | +22 to +24 | **Top 40 required** | Top 35 | The steepest cliff â€” AdjEM must jump ~8 points from R32 |
| Reach Elite 8 | +20+ (90% of teams) | Top 30 (73%) | Top 30 (71%) | Two-way competence required; one-dimensional teams get exposed |

**The R32 â†’ Sweet 16 cliff is the most important threshold in the bracket.** That ~8 AdjEM jump (from +14 to +22) is steeper than any other round transition. Teams in the +14 to +21 range will advance based on matchup; teams above +22 have a structural advantage.

### A2 â€” Why Chalk Dominates Each Round (And When It Doesn't)

**Why 1-4 seeds dominate**: Seeds 1â€“5 hold 72.5% of Sweet 16 spots, 66% of Elite 8 spots, and 54% of Final Four spots. This is not committee luck â€” it is efficiency correlation. The seeding committee's primary input is NET/KenPom rank, which directly predicts advancement probability. Seeds reflect the underlying data.

**Why the 6-9 dead zone exists**: Seeds 6-9 produced just 7.5% of Sweet 16 spots across 5 years â€” a structural bracket problem. 6-seeds face KenPom-30 11-seeds in R32. 7-seeds draw difficult R32 matchups. 8-9 seeds win a coin-flip in R1, then face a 1-seed in R32 (85-90% win rate). The path is structurally near-impossible without elite efficiency.

**Why 11-seeds appear in Sweet 16 every year**: Almost all are power conference at-large teams mis-assigned to 11 by the committee â€” KenPom 25-40 programs seeded low due to perception bias. Ohio State (2019), UCLA (2021, KenPom top-20), Iowa State (2022), Michigan (2022), NC State (2024, won the ACC Tournament) â€” these aren't Cinderellas, they're mis-seeds.

**Why chalk breaks down in specific spots**:
- **R64 5v12**: AdjEM gap averages only ~9 points â€” the smallest gap in the bracket below the 8/9 game. Market overvalues seeding; efficiency often shows near-parity.
- **R64 6v11**: 11-seeds cover 63.5% ATS because they're frequently better teams by efficiency. The "upset" is a seeding error being corrected.
- **R32 after Cinderella wins**: 12-15 seeds that won R1 are 0-10 ATS in R32 since 1998. The R64 win was matchup-specific; R32 opponents are prepared and better.

**The unbroken gate**: KenPom top 6 + AdjO top 25 + AdjD top 25. No champion outside this profile exists in the KenPom era.

## Part 5 â€” Statistical Profile of Contenders

### Sweet 16 Statistical Thresholds

**KenPom Adjusted Efficiency:**
- Teams in the **top 25 KenPom overall** reach the Sweet 16 at ~70-80%
- Teams outside the **top 50 KenPom** almost never reach Sweet 16 regardless of seed
- Every national champion in the KenPom era ranked in the **top 10 overall**

**The "Dual Elite" Profile:**
> Top 25 adjusted offensive efficiency AND top 25 adjusted defensive efficiency
> ~80%+ Sweet 16 rate. All 6 champions fit this profile.

**Offense vs. Defense:**
- Both ends matter, but **offense has become more important** in the modern era
- All 6 champions ranked **top 15 offensively** at tournament time
- Defense is the **floor** â€” teams with sub-top-50 defenses rarely survive two rounds
- Defense threshold: **Top 30-40 = reliable Sweet 16 contender**
- Offense threshold: **Top 40 = Sweet 16 viable, top 50 = minimum**

**Tempo/Pace:**
- No single tempo profile dominates for power programs
- For mid-major Cinderellas: **10 of 11 teams ranked outside the top 100 in adjusted tempo**
- Slow tempo equalizes talent gaps in single-elimination games
- Optimal Cinderella range: 65-72 possessions/game

**Turnover Rate:**
- Sweet 16 teams commit turnovers on **fewer than 17% of possessions**
- Forced turnover rate is an equalizer for mid-majors â€” 15%+ opponent TO rate signals upset potential
