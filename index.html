Round-by-Round

Why Teams Win at Each Stage

The statistical reason each round produces the results it does ‚Äî not narrative, not seeding, just efficiency data.

R64 ¬∑ First Round

Defense Is the Floor

AdjD top 40 is required for any Sweet 16+ run. The one exception ‚Äî Oral Roberts 2021 (AdjD #285) ‚Äî won on elite offense and exited the moment they faced a real defense. One-game variance allows luck; the floor matters from R32 onward.

Upset rate: 5‚Äì7 per year ¬∑ 5v12 upsets in every year studied
R32 ¬∑ Second Round

Regression Hits Cinderellas Hard

12-15 seed R64 wins rely on AdjEM gaps smaller than expected ‚Äî not genuine quality. In R32, the opponent is comparably strong and has full film on the Cinderella's tendencies. Result: 12-15 seeds are 0-10 ATS in R32 since 1998.

Chalk ATS: 68.75% in 2024 ¬∑ 10/11 seeds after R64 win: 13-5 ATS since 2016
Sweet 16

The 6‚Äì9 Dead Zone

Seeds 6‚Äì9 produced only 7.5% of Sweet 16 spots ‚Äî pure bracket position, not talent. 6-seeds draw KenPom-30 eleven-seeds in R32. 8/9 seeds who survive R1 face a 1-seed. The path is structurally punishing by design.

11-seeds appeared: Every single year ¬∑ Almost all were KenPom top 35
Elite 8

Two-Way or Bust

87.5% of Elite 8 teams had both AdjO and AdjD inside the national top 30. One-dimensional teams get fully scouted and attacked at their weakness. The "Extreme Team" warning: top-10 one metric + outside top-50 other = zero championships in 22 years.

Defense-first ceiling: Elite 8 ¬∑ Beyond here, offense limitations end runs
Final Four

The Three Paths to a Title

Efficiency dominance (UConn √ó2 ‚Äî simply better on both ends). Two-way balance + variance absorber (Virginia, Florida ‚Äî elite defense + free throw rate absorbs close games). Depth + adaptability (Kansas, Baylor ‚Äî bench and adjustment capacity).

Final Four winner covers championship: 83% ATS (35-7-2 since 2001)
Championship

The Gate Has Never Been Broken

Every champion 2019‚Äì2025 met all of: KenPom top 6, AdjEM +25+, AdjO top 25, AdjD top 25. UConn 2024 was the most dominant single-tournament performance ever recorded ‚Äî +36.4 AdjEM, +140 point differential across 6 games, won by 13+ every time.

Average champion AdjEM: +31.5 ¬∑ Average seed: 1.5
Cinderella Archetypes

Three distinct types of Cinderellas ‚Äî each with a hard ceiling determined by their statistical profile.

üõ°Ô∏è

Defense-First

Ceiling: Elite 8

AdjD top 30, weak AdjO. Keeps games close with every opponent. Exits when they finally face a team with both elite AdjO and elite AdjD ‚Äî the offense can't respond.

‚öñÔ∏è

Balanced

Ceiling: Final Four

Both AdjO and AdjD inside top 60. No exploitable weakness for opponents to attack. Can beat anyone in a single game. Rarely has the elite efficiency floor to win a title.

üî•

Offense-First

Ceiling: R32

AdjO top 25, weak AdjD. Works against a vulnerable favorite (see: Oral Roberts over Ohio State). Fails in R32 when the next opponent actually has a real defense.
